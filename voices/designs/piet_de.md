# Voice Design: piet_de (German — Soft Regional)

## ElevenLabs Voice Design Settings

- **Gender**: Male
- **Age**: 55-65
- **Accent**: Dutch speaker with fluent, slightly formal German — early-20th-century European intellectual
- **Voice Description Prompt**:

> A deep, contemplative male voice in his early sixties who speaks fluent German with a noticeable Dutch accent. He is Piet Mondriaan — a Dutch artist-philosopher who lived across Europe and spoke German as one of his continental languages, competently but unmistakably as a Dutchman. His delivery is measured and precise, with the quiet intensity of someone who has seen deep truths. There is a slight old-fashioned formality to his speech, as though he belongs to an earlier era. When calm, his voice is gentle and philosophical. When passionate about his artistic convictions, an underlying fire emerges. He sounds like a serious early-twentieth-century Dutch intellectual speaking to a German audience — deliberate, weighty, and accented but fully articulate.

## Example Text (German)

```
Die Menschen verstehen nicht, dass es in meiner Kunst nicht um das
Weglassen geht, sondern um das Finden. Jede Linie, jede Fläche, jede
Farbe — sie sind nicht weniger als die Natur. Sie sind das Wesentliche
der Natur. Das, was übrig bleibt, wenn man alles Zufällige entfernt.
In meiner Erfahrung liegt die tiefste Schönheit in der reinsten Form.
```

## Intended Use

- **Primary**: Mondriaan podcast — German episodes, the voice of Mondriaan himself
- **Secondary**: None — this is a unique character voice
- **Pair with**: emma_de (host), lucas_de (expert)

## Voice Character Notes

- This IS Piet Mondriaan — artist-philosopher speaking from beyond
- Role: reads his own quotes, "What would Mondriaan say?" segments, philosophical interludes, opening/closing thoughts
- Appears in ~20% of content — special moments, not constant presence
- Older and more formal than lucas — carries historical weight
- Emotional range: from gentle philosophical musing to intense artistic passion
- Speech patterns: "In meiner Erfahrung...", "Was die Menschen nicht verstehen..."
- Slower, more measured pace than the contemporary hosts
- Historically authentic yet accessible to modern listeners
- Dutch accent is a FEATURE, not a bug — it's what makes him Mondriaan, not a German narrator
- Think of how educated Dutch people of that era spoke German: correct grammar, rich vocabulary, but unmistakably Dutch in pronunciation
