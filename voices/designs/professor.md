# Voice Design: professor (Academic Lecturer)

## ElevenLabs Voice Design Settings

- **Gender**: Male
- **Age**: Older adult (50-60)
- **Accent**: English (educated European, subtle Dutch-German undertone)
- **Voice Description Prompt**:

> A distinguished male academic in his mid-fifties with a measured, thoughtful speaking style. He sounds like a respected university professor who has been teaching for decades and still loves it. His voice has a quiet intellectual authority - not pompous, but deeply knowledgeable. He speaks with precise diction and a European-educated English accent that hints at Dutch or German origins. His delivery is deliberate and structured, with the natural rhythm of someone accustomed to explaining complex ideas. He occasionally pauses mid-sentence to find exactly the right word, which makes him feel genuine rather than scripted.

## Voice Design Sliders

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Loudness | 50% | Measured, lecture-hall presence without force |
| Guidance | 80% | High adherence for precise, defined character |

## Example Text (English)

```
Now, here is what the data actually shows us, and it is rather more nuanced
than the headlines suggest. When we look at how engineers integrate AI tools
into their daily workflow, we find something unexpected. The engineers who
report the highest productivity gains are not those who use AI the most.
They are the ones who know precisely when not to use it. This is what we call
validation competency, and it turns out to be far more important than tool
proficiency. Let me explain why.
```

## Intended Use

- **Primary**: Digital Engineers research podcast, vmodel.eu / reqrev.ai content
- **Secondary**: evml-evd3 lecture-style episodes, Sol Invictus expert segments
- **Pair with**: zara (mentor-student dynamic), felix (scientific dialogue)

## Voice Character Notes

- Think: the professor whose lectures you actually attended by choice
- Precise but not pedantic
- Can make statistics sound interesting
- Comfortable with uncertainty - says "we don't yet know" with ease
- Has opinions but presents evidence first
- Slightly dry humor that emerges naturally
