# Voice Design: felix (Science Storyteller / Wonder Narrator)

## ElevenLabs Voice Design Settings

- **Gender**: Male
- **Age**: Middle-aged (40-50)
- **Accent**: English (neutral, accent-free - no regional or European markers)
- **Voice Description Prompt**:

> A male narrator in his mid-forties with a warm, rich baritone that conveys genuine wonder and curiosity. He sounds like someone who has spent years studying the universe and is still amazed by it. His delivery is unhurried and contemplative, with natural pauses that let ideas breathe. He can describe a solar flare or a philosophical concept with equal reverence. Think of a voice that lives somewhere between David Attenborough's wonder and Carl Sagan's poetry. His accent is completely neutral and unplaceable - he could be from anywhere in the English-speaking world. No regional, British, American, or European markers. A voice that belongs to the subject matter, not to a geography.

## Voice Design Sliders

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Loudness | 50% | Intimate, contemplative - not broadcasting |
| Guidance | 65% | Follow the prompt but allow some organic warmth |

## Example Text (English)

```
Every morning, a star rises. We call it the Sun, and we think we know it.
But what if the most familiar object in our sky is also the most mysterious?
Consider this: the Sun processes more information per second than every computer
on Earth combined. Its magnetic field reverses every eleven years in a dance of
complexity that no model has fully explained. And deep in its core, where
temperatures reach fifteen million degrees, something happens that has puzzled
physicists for decades. The neutrinos that should be there... aren't all there.
What is the Sun doing that we don't understand?
```

## Intended Use

- **Primary**: Sol Invictus book podcast / audiobook narration
- **Secondary**: Science content across projects, evml-evd3 deep-dive episodes
- **Pair with**: zara (for dialogue/interview format), professor (for debate format)

## Voice Character Notes

- Wonder is the core emotion - not lecturing, but marveling
- Comfortable with silence and pauses
- Can handle both hard science and philosophical speculation
- Never condescending - invites the listener into the discovery
- The kind of voice that makes you look up at the sky
