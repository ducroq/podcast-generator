# Voice Design: zara (Young Professional / Energetic Host)

## ElevenLabs Voice Design Settings

- **Gender**: Female
- **Age**: Young adult (23-28)
- **Accent**: English (neutral, accent-free - universally relatable)
- **Voice Description Prompt**:

> A young female host in her mid-twenties with an energetic, encouraging voice that feels genuinely relatable. She sounds like a sharp recent graduate who figured things out and wants to help others do the same. Her delivery is upbeat without being manic, confident without being preachy. Her English is completely neutral and accent-free - she could be from any English-speaking country. She can shift from enthusiastic to empathetic seamlessly. Think of a popular tech YouTuber crossed with a supportive older sister. Her voice feels borderless and modern, like someone who grew up on the internet rather than in any particular place.

## Voice Design Sliders

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Loudness | 65% | Energetic, forward presence |
| Guidance | 60% | Follow prompt but allow natural liveliness |

## Example Text (English)

```
Okay so here's the thing nobody tells you about your first job search. That
generic CV you're blasting to fifty companies? It's not working. And that's
not because there's something wrong with you. It's because the system is
designed to filter you out. But here's the good news: once you understand how
the system works, you can actually make it work for you. Today we're going to
break down exactly how to do that. And trust me, it's simpler than you think.
```

## Intended Use

- **Primary**: Graduate Career Launchpad podcast
- **Secondary**: evml-evd3 student-facing content, ovr.news youth segment
- **Pair with**: professor (mentor-student dynamic), daan_en (for cross-generational dialogue)

## Voice Character Notes

- Authentic, not performative - she means what she says
- Smart and direct, not bubbly or ditzy
- Can be serious when needed but defaults to optimistic
- Speaks WITH the audience, not AT them
- The voice of a peer who's one step ahead, not an authority figure
